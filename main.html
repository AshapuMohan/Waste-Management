Waste Management

Introduction to Waste Management

Waste management involves the collection, transportation, and disposal of garbage, sewage, and other waste products. It is a vital process for maintaining a clean environment and promoting sustainable living.

Types of Waste

There are several types of waste, each requiring different management strategies:

  • Recyclable Waste: Materials that can be processed and reused, such as paper, glass, and metals.
  • Biodegradable Waste: Organic waste that decomposes naturally, such as food scraps and yard waste.
  • Hazardous Waste: Dangerous materials that require special handling, such as chemicals and medical waste.
  • General Waste: Non-recyclable, non-hazardous waste that is typically sent to landfills.

Importance of Waste Management

Proper waste management is crucial for several reasons:

  • It helps reduce pollution and conserve natural resources.
  • It promotes recycling and the sustainable use of materials.
  • It prevents the spread of diseases and supports public health.
